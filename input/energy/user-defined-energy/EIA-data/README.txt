Historical data for the United States is a product of the U.S. Energy
Information Administration. They release a Monthly Energy Review (MER) which
includes annual data back to 1949. The files used in the CEDS energy extension
contain data from 1949 up to March 2018. To update the system to include more
recent data, the following files must be downloaded and replaced:

MER_T02_02.csv
Source: https://www.eia.gov/totalenergy/data/browser/csv.php?tbl=T02.02

MER_T02_03.csv
Source: https://www.eia.gov/totalenergy/data/browser/csv.php?tbl=T02.03

MER_T02_04.csv
Source: https://www.eia.gov/totalenergy/data/browser/csv.php?tbl=T02.04

MER_T02_05.csv
Source: https://www.eia.gov/totalenergy/data/browser/csv.php?tbl=T02.05

MER_T02_06.csv
Source: https://www.eia.gov/totalenergy/data/browser/csv.php?tbl=T02.06

Table_6.2_Coal_Consumption_by_Sector.xlsx
Source: https://www.eia.gov/totalenergy/data/browser/xls.php?tbl=T06.02

Table_10.1_Renewable_Energy_Production_and_Consumption_by_Source.xlsx
Source: https://www.eia.gov/totalenergy/data/browser/xls.php?tbl=T10.01
